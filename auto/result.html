14-Month-Old Milestones

Exploring the world with newfound independence.

Physical & Motor

  • Walking: Most are walking independently or "cruising" while holding furniture.
  • Fine Motor: Can stack two blocks and use a "pincer grasp" with precision.
  • Feeding: May attempt to use a spoon (though it’s messy!) and can drink from a cup.
  • Climbing: Starting to try to crawl up stairs or onto low furniture.

Cognitive & Learning

  • Imitation: Loves to copy daily chores (sweeping, talking on a phone, "dusting").
  • Following Directions: Can follow simple one-step commands like "Give me the ball."
  • Problem Solving: Experiments with cause and effect (dropping things to see them fall).
  • Object Permanence: Fully understands that things exist even when hidden.

Communication

  • Vocabulary: Usually says 1–3 simple words (like "Mama," "Dada," or "No").
  • Gestures: Points to show what they want or to get your attention.
  • Understanding: Recognizes names of familiar people and common objects.
  • Babbling: "Jargon" talk that sounds like real sentences but with nonsense words.

Social & Emotional

  • Separation Anxiety: May become clingy or cry when a parent leaves the room.
  • Affection: Shows hugs and kisses to familiar people and stuffed animals.
  • Independence: May start showing frustration or "tantrums" when they can't do something.
  • Social Play: Engages in "parallel play" (playing next to, but not with, other children).

When to Consult Your Pediatrician

Every child develops at their own pace. However, talk to your doctor if your 14-month-old:

  • Is not walking while holding onto things.
  • Does not point to objects.
  • Does not use at least a few words.
  • Loses skills they once had.
  • Doesn't seem to hear or respond to their name.
